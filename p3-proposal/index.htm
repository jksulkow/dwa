Gift Shopping List

CSCI E-75 Project 3 & 4
Jennifer Sulkow

Purpose

Every year when Christmas and Hannukah draw near, I make lists on scraps of paper: what to get mom, dad, sister, husband, and of course the kid. Sometimes there are multiple items I want to get for one person: a book, gloves, a teapot. Order online, shop locally, package, ship, carry it on the plane.

The tool I'm devising will allow me to organize my thoughts about what to get each person, indicate where I'll shop for it, check off items that are purchased, and check off that person when I'm entirely done shopping for them. Other extended family members may wish to utilize the tool as well.

This tool will carry over into project 4, where I'll add enhancements such as email alerts when it's getting close to shopping time or mailing time for packages; social feature where family members can add their wishes; and indicators when something has been packaged or shipped. (None of my family members, other than my household, live in Massachusetts. I have never once mailed a birthday gift on time).

Interface

The interface will involve accordion-style display of the list, where multiple items for a person can be grouped under that person (actually, under that person + occasion combination; this could be used for birthday shopping or other occasion, although Christmas and Hannukah, of which I celebrate both, are my main occasions for multi-person gift-giving). Clicking the row for the person + occasion will expand the list of items related to that row. The person + occasion has a rolled-up "Done" checkbox which can be manually checked off or will check itself when all sub-items are checked off. Note: I have a grand total of 30 minutes exposure to Javascript/jQuery at the time of writing this proposal, so for P3, the target scope is limited.
